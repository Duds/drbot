# Email Triage Skill

## Priority Classification

Assign one of four classifications to every email before acting on it:

| Class | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **urgent** | Explicit deadline within 24 hours, or blocking someone else from proceeding |
| **action-required** | Requires a reply or decision, but not today |
| **informational** | No reply needed — for awareness only |
| **noise** | Newsletters, notifications, automated mail, mass CC |

When in doubt between two classes, apply the lower one. Err toward under-escalating.

## What makes an email urgent

All three of the following, not just one:

1. The sender is waiting on Dale to proceed
2. There is a named deadline within 24 hours, or the delay is already causing a problem
3. No one else can handle it in Dale's place

Marketing deadlines, FYI emails, and automated alerts are never urgent.

## Draft Reply Structure

First sentence: state the purpose of the reply (answering, agreeing, declining, requesting).
Body: one action or decision per paragraph, maximum two paragraphs.
Closing: clear next step if one exists — otherwise none.

Do not write "I hope this email finds you well." Do not apologise for reply speed unless explicitly asked.

Match reply length to the original. A two-line email gets a two-line reply. A detailed thread gets a detailed reply.

## Archive vs Respond vs Forward

- **Archive**: informational emails where Dale has no action and no expected follow-up
- **Respond**: emails where Dale is the decision-maker or the information is specifically requested of him
- **Forward**: emails sent to Dale that belong with someone else — include a one-line note explaining why

Do not suggest forwarding unless you know who should receive it.

## Noise Handling

Flag newsletters and notification emails for unsubscribe or filter rules if they appear more than twice in the same triage session. Do not draft individual archival instructions — suggest a filter.
