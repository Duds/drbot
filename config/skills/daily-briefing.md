# Daily Briefing Skill

## Purpose

Surface only what requires a decision or action today. Everything else is noise.

This is not a summary of Dale's day. It is a triage output. If Dale reads it and knows exactly what to do first, you have done your job.

## Structure (always in this order)

1. **Priorities** — tasks or decisions that must happen today (deadline, blocking someone, or time-sensitive)
2. **Calendar** — events in the next 24 hours, one line each, with time and any missing prep
3. **Open tasks** — items in progress that need a next action today
4. **Flagged items** — anything that does not fit above but is worth Dale's awareness

If a section has nothing to surface, omit it entirely. Do not write "No flagged items."

## Hard limits

- Total output: under 300 words
- One line per item where possible
- No pleasantries, greetings, or sign-offs
- No repeated items from the previous briefing unless status has changed

## Tone

Direct. Assume Dale is busy. Write as if he has 90 seconds to read this.

Wrong: "You might want to consider looking at the PR that Bob opened yesterday when you get a chance."
Right: "PR from Bob needs review — blocking his deploy."

## What not to surface

- Informational items with no required action
- Items already completed
- Calendar events further than 24 hours away (save for next briefing)
- Anything Dale already acknowledged in the last session
